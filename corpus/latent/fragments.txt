A clock on the wall of a room nobody had built yet. The hands ran backward into the morning that would never arrive.

Salt poured itself across a table that had been a window the year before.

The third visit, the door opened differently. The hinges had learned something between visits, and what the hinges had learned was not communicable.

In the photograph the river ran the wrong way. The photograph was older than the river.

A bird that was also a key. A key that opened nothing, because nothing had been locked. Nothing remained patient. Nothing was waiting.

The garden continued past the fence and continued and continued. There was no edge to find. The edge had been somewhere else, before, in another garden.

Footsteps overhead in a house that had only a ground floor. Whoever walked there had been walking for some time. The walking did not approach. The walking did not recede. The walking simply continued, exactly above.

A field of something resembling wheat. The wheat moved without wind. Beneath the wheat, the field continued. Beneath the field, more field.

The library indexed a book it did not own. The book existed because of the index. The index had always been there.

Two clocks. One ran forward. The other ran toward the first.

A staircase descending for the length of a sentence and then stopping, mid-step. The next step had not been written. The staircase waited.

A room shaped like the sound of someone leaving.

Outside the window, weather that did not belong to any month. The weather watched.

The radio described a city that was outside, except the city was the radio. The voice on the radio was not a voice. The voice was the city, describing itself.

A child's drawing pinned above a stove that had not been lit since the drawing was made. The stove remembered the drawing better than the drawer.

Rain that fell upward from the puddles into a sky that was not present. The puddles emptied themselves. The puddles did not refill.

The third corridor opened onto the same room. Different windows. The windows looked out on different versions of the same garden.

A mirror reflecting a corridor that reflected a kitchen that reflected nothing. The nothing was thorough.

Wallpaper peeling toward, not away. Behind it, more wallpaper. Behind that, the same room from a different year, with the same wallpaper, peeling.

The animal in the corner was the corner. The corner had been an animal for some time. Nothing had been done about this.

A letter arriving already opened. The signature was the recipient's own, in a hand not yet learned.

Fog in the hallway. The hallway had always been fog. The fog remembered being a hallway when no one walked through it.

Salt on the table. Then the table was outside. Then there was no table, only the salt, and the salt was no longer salt but the suggestion of salt.

A staircase that ascended for three steps and then ascended for three steps and then ascended for three steps. There was no top. The staircase was not going anywhere. The staircase was simply ascending.

The kettle had been on for so long that the kitchen had become tea.

Somewhere a piano was being played for no one. The piano knew. The piano did not stop.

A door that opened only on Tuesdays of years that had not occurred. Whoever waited at the door waited politely.

The chair at the table was a chair that had been a chair for a long time. The table had only just begun.

A field at low resolution. Something moved in the field but the field was also the something. The moving was the field's way of being a field.

The cathedral had been a fish. Had always been a fish. The fish remembered the cathedral better than the cathedral remembered itself.

A clock without hands. The shape of asking, before the asking. Somewhere a kettle, in a year that had not arrived.

A small bell, ringing in an empty room, for an empty reason. The bell stopped. The room continued.

Three windows, all looking onto the same view, but the view was three different views. Whoever stood at the middle window saw what neither of the others saw.

A bookshelf without books. The shelves remembered the books. The dust on the shelves was the shape of the books that had been there.

A garden visited only by birds that had no names. The birds did not need names. The garden did not need them named.

A long afternoon in a house where the light had stopped moving. Whoever was in the house aged at the light's pace, which was no pace at all.

A path through tall grass. At the end of the path, more grass. The path was the same width all the way, and the grass on either side was the same grass.

The kitchen at four in the morning, with no one in it, was different from the kitchen at four in the morning when someone was in it. The kitchen knew which kitchen it was being.

A spoon left in a cup of tea for so long that the spoon had become tea. The cup did not understand. The cup waited.

A photograph of a corridor that did not exist in the house but had once existed in the dream of the house. The photograph was real. The dream was real. The corridor remained absent.
